# Product Guidelines

## Voice and Tone
- **Professional & Trustworthy:** Communications should instill confidence in the platform's reliability, especially regarding financial management.
- **Respectful & Community-Oriented:** The language should be respectful of Islamic traditions and values, fostering a sense of community.
- **Clear & Concise:** Instructions and labels should be easy to understand for users with varying levels of technical proficiency.

## Visual Identity
- **Clean & Modern:** A minimalist design that reduces clutter and focuses on task completion.
- **Accessible:** High contrast and clear typography to ensure usability for all age groups.
- **Calm Color Palette:** Use colors that evoke peace and trust (e.g., greens, blues, whites) consistent with mosque aesthetics.

## User Experience (UX) Principles
- **Simplicity First:** Prioritize core tasks like recording donations or scheduling events; avoid overwhelming the user with unnecessary options.
- **Transparency:** Ensure that all data (especially financial) is presented clearly and is easily auditable.
- **Mobile-First Design:** The platform should be fully functional on mobile devices to support users on the go.
- **Guided Workflows:** Use clear steps and feedback for complex actions to prevent errors.

## Code Quality Standards
- **Maintainability:** Write clean, documented code that is easy for other developers to understand and extend.
- **Security:** Prioritize data protection and secure authentication practices.
- **Performance:** Ensure fast load times and responsive interactions, even on slower connections.
